Departments

The Ministry of Health and the Ministry of Education are two key government departments, responsible for improving the well-being and development of the nation.
While the Ministry of Health focuses on providing medical services, disease prevention, and public health programs, the Ministry of Education oversees learning institutions, curriculum development, and training standards to ensure quality education for all citizens.

Ministry of Health

A health department is a government agency, operating at local, state, or national levels, tasked with protecting and promoting the health of the general public. Acting as the foundation for public health, these departments focus on preventing disease, managing health policies, and ensuring environmental safety. Their core functions include monitoring community health status, investigating health hazards, enforcing safety regulations, and providing essential services such as immunizations and maternal care. By addressing both communicable and noncommunicable disease risks, health departments work to improve overall population well-being, often acting as a safety net for vulnerable, uninsured, or underserved populations

Ministry of Education Employees

The Ministry of Education (MOE) formulates and implements education and training policies,
standards, curricula, examinations and the granting of university charters.
It is also responsible for the management of universities, tertiary educational institutions
and other institutions of research and higher learning and primary schools, special education,
secondary schools and special education institutions.
